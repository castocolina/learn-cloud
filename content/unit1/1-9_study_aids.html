Flashcards: Observability

Question

What is the primary goal of observability in cloud-native systems?

Answer

Observability helps you understand *why* a system is broken by allowing you to ask arbitrary questions about its internal state, unlike traditional monitoring which tells you *if* something is broken.

Question

What are the three pillars of observability?

Answer

The three pillars of observability are Logs, Metrics, and Traces.

    A[Observability] --> B(Logs);
    A --> C(Metrics);
    A --> D(Traces);

Question

Why is structured logging preferred over unstructured text logs in distributed systems?

Answer

Structured logging (e.g., JSON format) makes logs machine-readable, easier to parse, query, and analyze, which is crucial for managing logs in complex distributed systems.

Question

What is Distributed Tracing and what problem does it solve?

Answer

Distributed Tracing tracks the journey of a single request as it propagates through multiple services in a distributed system, helping to identify performance bottlenecks and debug complex interactions.

Question

What is OpenTelemetry?

Answer

OpenTelemetry is a vendor-neutral set of APIs, SDKs, and tools for instrumenting, generating, collecting, and exporting telemetry data (traces, metrics, and logs).
