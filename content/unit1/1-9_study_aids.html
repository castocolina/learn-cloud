1.9 Study Aids: Observability

Use these flashcards to review the key concepts of observability in cloud-native systems.

Observability vs. Monitoring

What is the key difference between observability and traditional monitoring?

Answer

Monitoring tells you if something is broken (e.g., CPU usage is high). Observability helps you understand why it's broken by allowing you to ask arbitrary questions about the system's internal state from its external outputs.

Three Pillars of Observability

Name the three pillars of observability.

Answer

The three pillars of observability are Logs, Metrics, and Traces.

Structured Logging

What is structured logging, and what is its main benefit?

Answer

Structured logging involves outputting logs in a machine-readable format (e.g., JSON) with key-value pairs. Its main benefit is making logs easier to parse, query, and analyze programmatically, especially in distributed systems.

Prometheus Metrics

What are metrics in the context of observability, and what is Prometheus used for?

Answer

Metrics are numerical measurements collected over time (e.g., request rates, error counts). Prometheus is an open-source monitoring system that collects and stores these metrics as time series data, allowing for powerful querying and alerting.

Distributed Tracing

What problem does distributed tracing solve in microservices architectures?

Answer

Distributed tracing solves the problem of understanding the flow and performance of a single request as it travels through multiple services. It helps pinpoint latency issues, identify service dependencies, and debug complex interactions across a distributed system.
